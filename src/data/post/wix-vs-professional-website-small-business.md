If you’ve started looking into getting a website, you’ve almost certainly seen the adverts: build your own in an afternoon, from a few pounds a month, no designer needed.

Wix, Squarespace and GoDaddy are genuinely clever tools, and for some businesses they’re the right choice. But “cheap and easy to start” and “right for your business” aren’t the same thing — and the difference usually shows up a year or two down the line, not on day one.

This guide compares Wix (as a stand-in for all the DIY builders) against a professionally built website, in plain terms, so you can decide which one actually fits where your business is right now.

---

## The honest case for Wix

Let’s be fair to it. For the right business, a DIY builder is a sensible call.

- **It’s cheap to start.** Roughly £10–£30 a month, with hosting and security bundled in.
- **You’re in control.** Want to change your opening hours or add a photo at 9pm? You can, without emailing anyone.
- **It’s quick.** A basic site can be live in a weekend.
- **It’s low-risk for testing an idea.** If you’re not yet sure the business will fly, spending thousands on a website is premature.

If you’re brand new, on a near-zero budget, and just need a simple online presence so people can find your phone number — Wix is often the right starting point. Don’t let anyone talk you out of it.

---

## Where the DIY route quietly costs you

The problems with builders aren’t usually obvious upfront. They creep in.

### 1) Your time is the real price
The “£15 a month” number ignores the most expensive ingredient: your hours. Learning the editor, wrestling with layouts, rewriting the copy three times, fixing the bit that looks broken on mobile — that’s an evening here and a Sunday there. If your time is worth anything to your business, the saving shrinks fast.

### 2) It usually looks like a template — because it is one
Customers can tell. A site built from the same template as thousands of others sends a quiet signal that you’re one of many, not the obvious choice. A professional site is built around *your* services, *your* photos and the questions *your* customers actually ask.

### 3) Speed and SEO are often weaker
DIY builders add a lot of behind-the-scenes code to stay flexible, and that tends to make pages heavier and slower. Speed matters — a slow site loses enquiries and gets ranked lower by Google. Getting a builder-based site to load quickly and rank well is possible, but it’s fiddly, and most people never get to it.

### 4) You hit a ceiling
Everything’s fine until you want something the builder doesn’t do neatly — a proper booking flow, a specific layout, a faster quote form. Then you’re fighting the tool instead of using it.

---

## What “professionally built” actually gets you

Paying a studio or a good freelancer isn’t about buying more pages. You’re buying skilled work you’d otherwise have to do yourself — or do without.

- **A design built for your business,** not a template you pour your details into.
- **A site built to convert,** with clear headlines, obvious calls-to-action, trust signals and fast enquiry forms — the things that turn visitors into phone calls.
- **Speed and technical SEO handled properly,** so Google can find you and pages load fast on a phone on 4G.
- **Someone to ask.** When you need a change or something breaks, there’s a person who knows your site.

The trade-off is honest: it costs more upfront, and you’re relying on someone else for big changes. For most established businesses, that’s a fair deal — the site earns its keep in enquiries.

## The comparison, side by side

| | Wix / DIY builder | Professionally built |
|---|---|---|
| **Upfront cost** | Low (£10–£30/month) | Higher one-off (typically £1,500–£5,000) |
| **Your time** | High | Low |
| **Look** | Template-based | Built around your business |
| **Speed & SEO** | Often weaker | Handled properly |
| **Changes** | You do them | Usually done for you |
| **Ceiling** | You’ll hit one | Room to grow |

Neither column is “wrong”. They suit different businesses at different stages.

---

## Do you really own it?

This is the question people forget to ask, and it matters most.

With most DIY builders, your site is locked to their platform. You can’t simply pick it up and move it elsewhere — if you leave, you generally start again. Some cheap “done for you” packages are worse: you rent the site monthly and own nothing, so the day you stop paying, it disappears.

Before you commit to *any* option, ask one thing: **do I own this, and can I take it with me?** A professionally built site should be yours — no monthly lock-in, no hostage situation.

---

## So which should you choose?

A simple way to decide:

- **Brand new, tiny budget, testing whether the business works?** Start with Wix. It’s the sensible, low-risk move. Upgrade once the enquiries are coming in.
- **Established, relying on word-of-mouth, but losing jobs to better-looking competitors?** A professional fixed-price site usually pays for itself in a handful of jobs — and stops costing you your evenings.
- **Somewhere in between?** Be honest about your time. If “I’ll build it myself this weekend” has been true for six months, that’s your answer.

The expensive mistake isn’t choosing Wix or choosing an agency — it’s paying twice. Spending months on a DIY site that never brings in work, then rebuilding it properly anyway, costs far more than getting it right once.
